My Web Development Journey

At some point when I was a little kid, my uncle Bret purchased me a domain with my very own name!
Unbeknownst to me, this would propel me into a fascination with computers, wesbites, and how exactly what was emerging as Web2.0 worked.
I was (un)fortunate enough to be born in an era where the internet was convienent, easily accessible, and tailored to the consumer experience.
However, this created an interesting dilemma in which modern web design seemed unattainable, at least in the face of old sites from a decade before my time
The summer before I began middle school, my uncle informed me of the domain I had in my name (JacobAlteri.com).
I was fascinated with the idea of being able to show my friends my very own Wesbite!
The only thing it contained for years was "Hey Jacob, call your uncle when you see this".
That summer I spent so much time attempting to write a cool and unique website, and to show it off when I went back to school.
I wish I still had an image of that original site!
After that, my love of coding and designing dimished. It was simply too confusing for me to understand anything beyond basic HTML as a kid.
As time went on, I pursued coding in small little obsessive spells.
Designing a Snake Game in Python, learning small tidbits here and there of C level language to get games to work in Unity,
engaging in coding competitions hosted by UK (The college I eventually enrolled at!) all kept my love for coding there, but at a proverbial distance.
Coding had always been a fun little hobby to me, but it wasn't until highschool that I really started to view it as a possible career option.


For me, coding and computer science gives me the ability to create something tangible to be proud of,
which not many professions can offer while also engaging in a high level of theoretical ideas.
This is why I so intimately love Computer Science, and do what I do.
